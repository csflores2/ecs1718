Famous Social Activist

Rosa Parks

"The Mother of the Civil Rights Movement"

By refusing to give up her seat to a white man on a Montgomery, Alabama city bus in 1955, Rosa Parks helped initiate the civil rights movement in the U.S.


Rosa Parks was born in Tuskegee, Alabama on February 4, 1913.


She wrote an autobiography, "Rosa Parks: My Story." and was awarded the Congressional Gold Metal, the highest honor the U.S. bestows on a civilian.
